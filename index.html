Bear cub rescue & Bears' Print news inside!
You won't stop us COVID! On May 7th we rescued 2 tiny female moon bear cubs in Laos. COVID restrictions had prevented the rescue 2 weeks prior so we had to get creative. Watch the Bridge of Cubs handover! 
HAPPY MOTHER'S DAY!

Dear <<First Name>>, A very, beary Happy Mother's Day to all the wonderful mothers in the world, including all of our wonderful Free the Bears supporters. Thank you for all that you do.

Many of the bears we rescue have been orphaned as cubs due to poaching & the illegal wildlife trade. It is only thanks to your support that these babies are able to be given the loving expert care that allows them to recover, learn to trust again & have a second chance at a decent life. 

Thanks to you, they are given a new family, the Free the Bears family. In our eyes, all of our supporters are mothers to these orphaned bears. On that note, we have some very exciting news. Congratulations, you have 2 more children!

On Friday May 7 we were finally able to rescue the 2 bear cubs we'd heard of weeks ago but were prevented from rescuing due to a strict nationwide lockdown in Laos following a major COVID outbreak.

 Because of the lockdown, we had to get creative with the rescue. Our government partners from Xayaboury Department of Forest Inspection made the long journey to confiscate the cubs. We met in the middle of a bridge which marked the provincial border (& couldn't be crossed) for the handover. With the mighty Mekong river & mountain ranges in the background, it was all very dramatic, reminiscent of a spy movie...Bridge of Cubs perhaps!?!

The tiny female cubs, sisters, weigh only 5kg & are estimated to be just 4 months old. They're safely back at the cub nursery of our Luang Prabang Wildlife Sanctuary & on the road to recovery. Please enjoy the video with highlights of the past 2 days.

We've also recently rescued 3 endangered species in Laos - an Indochinese grey langur with terrible wounds (listed as Endangered on the IUCN Red List), a baby Sunda pangolin (listed as Critically Endangered) & a baby stump-tailed macaque (list as Vulnerable). We're hopeful that they'll all make a full recovery, thanks to your ongoing support.

MARY GETS LOCKED UP (AGAIN)!

Our fearless founder Dr Mary Hutton OAM has been fighting for over 26 years to end the suffering of sun bears & moon bears in bile farms & the illegal wildlife trade. On May 29th Mary plans to spend an uncomfortable 'Night In A Cage' to raise awareness & funds to rescue & care for bears in need. 

Despite being in her 80s & suffering from pain herself, Mary will tell you that her suffering is nothing compared to that of the bears.

Please sponsor Mary for her Night In A Cage, we'd very much appreciate it.
